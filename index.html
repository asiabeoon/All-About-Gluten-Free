Pros:

  1. May relieve symptoms of celiac disease and gluten sensitivity
  2. lead to an increase in nutrient-dense whole foods, such as fruits and vegetables
  3. Can lead to weight loss as gluten-free foods often contain fewer calories and less sugar and fat than their gluten-containing counterparts

    4. Cons:

  4. Can be more expensive as gluten-free products are often pricier than regular products
  5. May be lower in fiber, iron, and B vitamins due to the removal of fortified grains and cereals
  6. May limit food choices and make it more challenging to eat out or find convenient, on-the-go options.
